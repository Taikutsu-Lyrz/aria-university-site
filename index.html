About Aria University

Aria University is committed to academic excellence and the future of its students. We offer diverse programs, expert faculty, and a campus that promotes growth.

Aria University in Mazar-i-Sharif is one of the more respected private universities in northern Afghanistan, known especially for its key programs in Bachelor of Computer Science (BCS), Bachelor of Medicine, and Bachelor of Economics . These three departments attract the majority of students because they offer clear paths to practical, -demand careers in a country where digital skills, healthcare, and economic development are critically needed. The BCS program caters to a growing interest in technology and software, giving students a shot at entering both local tech spaces and remote global opportunities. The Bachelor of Medicine program, although challenged by resource limitations, draws students who want to directly address the country's urgent medical shortages and contribute to saving lives. Meanwhile, the Economics department trains future thinkers and planners to tackle Afghanistan’s complex financial landscape, from grassroots entrepreneurship to national policy reform. Despite its limited infrastructure and the broader instability in the region, Aria University continues to provide a sense of direction for hundreds of students who might otherwise have no access to higher education. The university's campus may lack luxury, but its students carry grit. Many juggle classes with part-time jobs or family duties, determined to change their futures. The professors—some with strong academic backgrounds, others more traditional—form the backbone of the institution, doing their best in a system that doesn’t always have their back. Yet, amid power cuts, outdated facilities, and occasional administrative chaos, a real sense of purpose drives everyone. Aria University might not be perfect, but in a city like Mazar-i-Sharif, it's a symbol of hope, ambition, and survival through education.
